ADA Service Dog Definition

Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), a service animal is:

"A dog that has been individually trained to do work or perform tasks for an individual with a disability. The task(s) performed by the dog must be directly related to the person's disability."

Your Rights as a Veteran

As a disabled Marine Corps veteran, you may be eligible for:

  • VA veterinary health insurance benefits for service dogs
  • Coverage of premiums, copayments, and deductibles
  • Access to veteran-specific training programs (Dogs4Vets allows training your own dog)
  • Consultation with VA clinical care providers for service dog evaluation
